Welcome to Red Barn!

Make your yard the envy of the neighborhood with flowers and plants from our native plant nursery and garden center in Austin, Tx. Red Barn Garden Center is a full-service retail nursery proudly serving the community since 1978. With three acres of land and an attractive facility featuring an extensive selection of annual plants, fruit trees, and planting materials, we have everything you need to fill your yard with color. Contact us today at (512) 335-8093 in Austin, Texas, to speak with our knowledgeable staff about your gardening questions.

November is a great time to plant Trees and Shrubs.

Planting shrubs and trees with fruit nuts or berries is an excellent food source for the birds and other wildlife. Some of the best shrubs for Central Texas are American Beautyberry, Agarita, Burford Holly, Possumhaw Holly, Yaupon Holly, Cherry Laurel, Pyracantha, Leatherleaf Mahonia, and Nandina. Plant shrubs and trees now to give them a chance to establish before the next hot summer.

History of Red Barn

Red Barn. The name itself brings visions of pastures, serenity and blue skies. Red Barn Garden Center, now located on Pond Springs Road just north of Spicewood Springs Road (in the old community of Jollyville), rests on the old Thompson homestead.
Owner Emelie McDaniel says, “People feel the history when they wander around the nearly four acres, sheltered by the big old oaks. The oaks were huge when Mr. Thompson was a mere child here almost a century ago. Our visitors feel relaxed and enjoy being with the plants. Our goal when we moved south onto this land in 2001 was to recreate the peace and serenity you feel in a nice garden, with the ease to get in and out. After the horrific tragedy that struck our nation on Sept. 11, 2001, people came and walked around just for the comfort this place offers. People came on their lunch hour and wandered around. The following month we held the largest Pumpkin Fest we’ve ever seen; families needed to connect to a quitter place in time,” said McDaniel.

Red Barn was founded by Scottie Gosh in 1978. He taught McDaniel his gardening secrets, such as growing organics and xeriscaping. When he was ready to sell the business in 1993 he turned to McDaniel.
The property that houses Red Barn has always been home to green businesses-although they were putting greens. Holes from the Green Acres putt-putt golf course still dot the land and lend Red Barn some of its appeal, not to mention a perpetual lawn of lush green AstroTurf.
